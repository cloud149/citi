Savings Account FAQs

We have the answers to your savings account questions.

  • A savings account is a bank account that pays you interest, helping you save for short- and long-term goals – from vacations to college funds to down payments. FDIC-insured up to allowable limits, a savings account is a safe way to hold on to your money while helping it grow.

    In addition, you can transfer funds online between your Citi savings account and other linked Citi accounts or non-Citi accounts. You also can access available cash from your savings account at over 65,000 fee-free ATMs.12

    To learn more, visit Citi's Your Savings Made Simple learning center.

  • A savings account is a valuable tool to help you start saving money. Designed to grow your money and provide easy access, savings accounts are an ideal place to store an emergency fund, as you can be confident your money will be available when you need it.

    Opening a savings account is also a great way to save for large purchases such as moving, buying a car, or taking a dream vacation.

  • At Citi, your checking and savings accounts will be opened in an account package that offers services and benefits to meet your banking needs. You also can avoid monthly service fees by meeting the account package combined average balance requirements. To learn more about Citi's different account packages and pricing, see the Compare Account Packages Chart.

  • You can apply online in minutes to open a savings account with Citi.1 Your savings account will be opened within a package that determines interest rates, balance requirements, benefits and more. Just select an account package, enter your personal information, verify your information and choose funding options. You'll get an email once your application is received, plus a follow-up email (along with any next steps, if applicable) telling you if your savings account opening request has been approved.

  • A new Citibank customer who opens a stand-alone Citi savings account online, by phone or in a branch will be mailed an ATM card once the account application is approved.

  • Yes, you can link your savings account to any of the following Citi accounts:

    • Checking
    • CDs and IRAs
    • Investment account
    • Credit card
    • Mortgage
    • Home equity line and loan
    • Personal loan

  • Absolutely! You can open as many savings accounts as you like. Many of our customers open several separate accounts for specific savings goals like vacations, holiday shopping and unexpected expenses. You can do the same and set up Auto Save to make automatic transfers to your savings accounts. You also can nickname your savings accounts to match your various objectives.

    See Citi's Financial Planning Tools page, for calculators to help you plan for retirement, save for major purchases and other financial goals.
